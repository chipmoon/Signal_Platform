## 📊 1. Core Market Indicators

### **ATR (Average True Range)**
*   **What it is**: A measure of **volatility**. It calculates the average range a price moves over a set period (usually 14 days).
*   **How to read it**:
    *   **High ATR**: The market is "noisy" and volatile. You should use wider Stop Losses.
    *   **Low ATR**: The market is calm or consolidating.
*   **Usage**: We use ATR to calculate "Volatility-Adjusted Position Sizing." If a stock has a high ATR, the system will suggest a smaller position size to keep your financial risk the same.

### **COT (Commitment of Traders)**
*   **What it is**: Data from the CFTC (USA) showing the net positions of different types of traders in the futures market.
*   **Categories**:
    *   **Commercials**: Industry players (hedgers). Usually the "Smart Money."
    *   **Non-Commercials**: Large speculators (Hedge Funds).
*   **How to read it**:
    *   **Net Long Positive**: Speculators are bullish.
    *   **Extreme Divergence**: When specs are extremely long and commercials are extrêmement short, a market reversal is often near.
*   **Usage**: Used for high-level sentiment analysis on Commodities (Gold, Oil) and Currencies.

## 📈 2. Technical Momentum & Trend

### **RSI (Relative Strength Index)**
*   **Scale**: 0 to 100.
*   **Overbought (>70)**: The price may have risen too fast and is due for a pullback or consolidation.
*   **Oversold (<30)**: The price may have fallen too fast and is due for a bounce.
*   **Usage**: AI uses RSI to detect "exhaustion" in price trends.

### **MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)**
*   **What it is**: A trend-following momentum indicator.
*   **Crossovers**: When the MACD line crosses above the Signal line, it's a **Bullish** signal.
*   **Divergence**: If price makes a new high but MACD doesn't, the trend is weakening.

## 🛡️ 4. Risk Management Basics

*   **Drawdown**: The percentage drop from the highest peak of your portfolio. Managing drawdown is more important than chasing returns.
*   **Position Sizing**: The system calculates how many shares you should buy based on your total capital and the stock's current ATR. **Never bet more than 1-2% of your capital on a single trade.**
